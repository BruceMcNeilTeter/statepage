Fort Robinson Nebraska Area

Fun at Fort Robinson

View from a cliff

Beautiful scenery surrounds Ft Robinson State Park. Rock out cropping and conifer trees make up the cliffs around the park.

Site of Chief Crazy Horse's death and jail where he was held

Chief Crazy Horse of the Ogallala Sioux tribe, was caught in the area, and he was imprisioned at the font. This building is a replica of the jail and is the site where Crazy Horse died while trying to resist confinement.

Officers quaters now rented to guest for overnight lodging.

The officers quaters are original buildings from years ago when Fort Robinson was a working fort. Now they are used for guests and can be rented for an economical fee to house your whole family.

Teepee located near park entrance

This teepee is located near the enterance to the park. Visitors have access to view it and look inside.
